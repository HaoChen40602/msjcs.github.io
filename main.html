Game Generator Algorithms

By qwerytiop

Game Algorithms! We briefly went over various ways of generating mazes, then went to Perlin noise. We covered the basic generation, interpolation (smoothing), and extension to multiple dimensions of Perlin noise. Perlin noise is used to generate terrains in games, and is also used to procedurally create textures and graphics that look more natural.

Arrays and Lists + PicoCTF info 9/30

By Val Choung

This time, we went over the different types of arrays and lists before info on PicoCTF (at picoctf.com.) We passed around a sign-up sheet for more info on PicoCTF. PicoCTF is a cybersecurity competition aimed at high school students. While competing, students can learn more about computer science/hacking. Students compete in a team, which must be affiliated with the school to be eligible for prizes. We'll have more info on signing up as MSJ-affiliated teams later! If you couldn't make the meeting, shoot us an email at msjhscompsci@gmail.com .
